## 4. Required disclosures by provider

The provider must disclose, in writing, the following:

1. AML/KYC license number and issuing regulator in their
   jurisdiction (e.g. BVI Financial Services Commission, Estonian
   Financial Intelligence Unit, UAE FSRA).
2. Insurance coverage for professional indemnity (minimum coverage
   in USD or EUR).
3. List of regulators they are registered with.
4. Number of active corporate clients (range; specifics under NDA).
5. Years of operation in the jurisdiction (NO menos de 5 anos para
   shortlisting).
6. Number of formal complaints / regulatory actions in the past 5
   years; nature and resolution of each.
7. Disclosure of any beneficial ownership obligations they have to
   tax authorities or regulators (CRS, FATCA, BOI etc.).
8. Their data retention policy (how long they keep KYC records and
   correspondence).
9. Whether they outsource any services and to which providers
   (must be disclosed for AML pass-through).
10. Their stated policy for responding to subpoenas, MLA requests,
    or law enforcement inquiries (timeline, notification to client
    if legally permitted).

## 6. Service level expectations (provider must accept or counter)

- Response to client emails: within 1 business day in jurisdiction
  business hours.
- Mail scanning: scanned within 2 business days of receipt at
  registered office.
- Filings deadline notification: 30 days before due date,
  reminder at 14 days, final reminder at 7 days.
- Subpoena / MLA notification: provider must commit to NOTIFYING
  client within 24 hours of receipt of any subpoena or MLA request,
  to the extent legally permitted in the jurisdiction.
- Termination notice: provider must allow client termination with
  30 days notice; client must commit to 30 days notice and to
  appointing a successor agent within those 30 days.

## 7. Nominee director declaration (required addendum)

If nominee director services are offered, the provider must agree
to sign a Nominee Director Declaration with the following effects:

1. The nominee director acts solely on instructions from the
   beneficial owner ("Client").
2. The nominee director has no autonomous authority to:
   - Open or close bank accounts
   - Transfer funds
   - Enter into contracts in excess of [USD 1,000]
   - Pledge or transfer the entity's assets
   - File for dissolution or restructuring
3. The nominee director undertakes to act in accordance with
   written instructions communicated through a designated secure
   channel (PGP-encrypted email or equivalent agreed channel).
4. The Client indemnifies the nominee director for liabilities
   arising from instructions properly given.
5. The nominee director may decline instructions which are
   manifestly illegal in their jurisdiction; in such case, the
   nominee must disclose to the Client and seek alternative
   arrangement.

## 9. Conflicts and disqualifying factors (RFP self-disclosure)

The Client requests that the provider disclose:

- Whether the provider currently acts for any entity in the music

  with the Client.
- Whether any of the provider's officers, directors, or beneficial
  owners are persons subject to OFAC sanctions or other
  international sanctions lists.
- Whether the provider is currently subject to any regulatory
  investigation in the jurisdiction.
