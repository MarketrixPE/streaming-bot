# NDA — Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (Contractor)

> Plantilla para subcontratistas (devs, ops, abogados, contadores,
> traductores, OTC desks, registered agents). Aplica jurisdiccion
> de la entity contratante.
>
> **Antes de firmar**: revisa con tu tax/contract lawyer en la
> jurisdiccion de la entity contratante. Esta plantilla es starting
> point operativo, NO un asset legal validado.

---

## MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

**This Agreement** (the "Agreement") is entered into as of
[Effective Date] by and between:

**Disclosing Party**: [Entity legal name],
a [Jurisdiction] [type of entity, e.g. Limited Liability Company]
with registered office at [registered office address]
("Company").

**Receiving Party**: [Contractor legal name],
[individual / entity], domiciled at [address], passport / corporate
registration [reference number]
("Contractor").

(Each a "Party"; collectively the "Parties").

---

### 1. PURPOSE

The Parties wish to discuss and potentially engage in a business
relationship in which the Contractor will provide
[describe services in 1-2 sentences, e.g. "software development
services for the Company's media distribution platform"]
(the "Purpose"). In connection with the Purpose, the Company
will disclose to the Contractor certain confidential information.

### 2. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means any information disclosed by the
Company to the Contractor, in any form (oral, written, electronic,
visual), that is identified as confidential at the time of
disclosure or that the Contractor should reasonably understand to
be confidential given the nature of the information and the
circumstances of disclosure. Without limitation, Confidential
Information includes:

- The Company's catalogue of musical works, related metadata, and
  distribution arrangements
- Identities of artists, including stage names, biographical data,
  imagery, contact information
- Identities of distributors, labels, sub-licensees, and platforms
  with which the Company has commercial relationships
- Account credentials, API keys, tokens, certificates, and
  cryptographic material of any kind
- IP addresses, hostnames, network topology, and infrastructure
  details
- Source code, configuration files, build artifacts, and technical
  documentation
- Banking information, financial accounts, transaction records
- Beneficial ownership information of the Company and its
  affiliates
- The existence and terms of this Agreement and of any related
  engagement
- The fact that the Contractor is providing services to the Company

### 3. EXCLUSIONS

Confidential Information does NOT include information which the
Contractor can demonstrate by written records:

(a) Was lawfully in the Contractor's possession prior to disclosure
by the Company without obligation of confidence;
(b) Is or becomes publicly available without breach of this
Agreement;
(c) Was rightfully received by the Contractor from a third party
without obligation of confidence;
(d) Was independently developed by the Contractor without use of or
reference to the Company's Confidential Information.

### 4. OBLIGATIONS

The Contractor shall:

(a) Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without
prior written consent of the Company; written consent must be
unambiguous and may be revoked by the Company at any time;
(c) Protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree
of care that the Contractor uses to protect its own most sensitive
confidential information, and in no event less than reasonable
care;
(d) Limit access to Confidential Information to the Contractor's
personnel and subcontractors who have a need-to-know for the
Purpose AND who are bound by written confidentiality obligations
no less restrictive than those in this Agreement;
(e) Notify the Company immediately upon discovery of any
unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information.

### 5. RESTRICTIONS ON USE OF SYSTEMS AND IDENTITY

The Contractor specifically agrees:

(a) Not to use Confidential Information to access, log in to,
modify, or extract data from any system, account, or platform of
the Company outside of the scope of work expressly agreed in
writing.
(b) Not to copy, photograph, screenshot, or otherwise record
Confidential Information except as strictly necessary for the
Purpose, and to delete all such copies upon completion of the
Purpose or termination of this Agreement.
(c) Not to disclose, on social media, public forums, blog posts, CV,
LinkedIn, GitHub, or any public channel, the existence of the
engagement or any aspect of the Purpose or Confidential Information.
(d) Not to attempt to identify or contact any artist, distributor,
counterparty, or other third party referenced in the Confidential
Information for any purpose other than the Purpose.

### 6. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION

Upon written request of the Company, or upon termination of the
engagement, the Contractor shall, within seven (7) days:

(a) Return to the Company all Confidential Information in tangible
form, OR at the Company's option, destroy such information.
(b) Permanently delete all electronic copies, including from
backups, version control history (rebase or prune as required),
cloud storage, local devices, and any third-party systems.
(c) Certify in writing that the foregoing has been completed,
identifying any exception (e.g. legal retention obligation) and
the basis for such exception.

### 7. NO LICENSE

Nothing in this Agreement grants the Contractor any rights, by
license or otherwise, to any of the Company's intellectual property
or Confidential Information except the limited right to use such
information for the Purpose.

### 8. WORK PRODUCT AND IP ASSIGNMENT

(a) All work product created by the Contractor in the course of
performing the Purpose, including all source code, designs,
documents, configurations, and derivative works
("Work Product"), shall be the exclusive property of the Company
upon creation.
(b) The Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all
right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product, including
all copyright, trade secret, and other intellectual property
rights, throughout the world, in perpetuity.
(c) The Contractor agrees to execute any further documents
necessary to perfect the assignment.
(d) The Contractor waives, to the extent permitted by law, any
moral rights in the Work Product.

### 9. NON-CIRCUMVENTION

For a period of twenty-four (24) months following termination of
the engagement, the Contractor shall not:

(a) Directly or indirectly solicit, contract with, or accept work
from any artist, distributor, sub-licensee, platform partner, or
other counterparty of the Company that the Contractor became aware
of through the Purpose, except with prior written consent of the
Company;
(b) Develop, operate, or assist in operating a competing business
that uses substantially the same techniques, methods, or
infrastructure design as those disclosed by the Company.

### 10. TERM

This Agreement is effective from the Effective Date and continues
for a period of five (5) years thereafter, except that the
obligations with respect to trade secrets and beneficial ownership
information shall continue indefinitely.

### 11. REMEDIES

The Contractor acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may
cause irreparable harm to the Company for which monetary damages
would be inadequate, and that the Company shall be entitled to
seek injunctive relief in addition to any other remedy available
at law or in equity.

In addition, the Contractor agrees to liquidated damages of
[USD 50,000 per breach] for breaches of Sections 4, 5, and 9, this
amount being a reasonable pre-estimate of damages and not a
penalty.

### 12. INDEMNIFICATION

The Contractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the
Company and its affiliates, officers, directors, and beneficial
owners from any claim, loss, or liability arising from:

(a) The Contractor's breach of this Agreement;
(b) The Contractor's violation of any applicable law in performing
the Purpose;
(c) Any claim by a third party engaged by the Contractor in
connection with the Purpose without the Company's written consent.

### 13. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

This Agreement is governed by the laws of [Jurisdiction of the
Company], without regard to conflict-of-law principles.

Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement
shall be finally resolved by arbitration administered by
[Arbitration body, e.g. London Court of International Arbitration
(LCIA), Singapore International Arbitration Centre (SIAC), or
ICC Paris] under its rules of arbitration. The seat of arbitration
shall be [city]. The language of arbitration shall be English.
The number of arbitrators shall be one (1) for disputes under
USD 250,000 and three (3) otherwise.

The Parties waive any right to a jury trial and to participate in
class actions.

### 14. NOTICES

All notices shall be in writing and delivered:

- To the Company at: [Company secure communications channel,
  e.g. PGP-encrypted email at legal@<entity-domain>]
- To the Contractor at: [Contractor email]

A notice is deemed received upon delivery confirmation.

### 15. MISCELLANEOUS

(a) **Entire Agreement**. This Agreement constitutes the entire
agreement of the Parties on its subject.
(b) **Amendments**. Amendments must be in writing signed by both
Parties.
(c) **Severability**. If any provision is unenforceable, the
remainder shall remain in effect.
(d) **No Waiver**. Failure to enforce a provision is not a waiver.
(e) **Assignment**. The Contractor shall not assign this Agreement
without prior written consent of the Company; the Company may
assign this Agreement to an affiliate or successor.
(f) **Counterparts**. This Agreement may be executed in
counterparts, including via electronic signature.

### 16. ANTI-LEAK CLAUSE

The Contractor specifically acknowledges:

(a) That information about the Company's beneficial ownership,
banking arrangements, jurisdictional structure, and operational
techniques constitutes both Confidential Information AND trade
secrets;
(b) That disclosure of such information to law enforcement, tax
authorities, journalists, or investigators (other than as required
by a valid subpoena addressed to the Contractor in their personal
capacity) is a material breach of this Agreement and may
constitute tortious interference with business relations;
(c) That the Contractor has been advised to seek independent legal
counsel before signing this Agreement and has had the opportunity
to do so.

## Operational notes for the operator (NOT part of NDA itself)

- DO NOT send the entity's beneficial ownership or banking
  information to the contractor BEFORE the NDA is signed and
  returned.
- For high-trust contractors (long-term devs with admin access to
  control plane), consider supplementing with a separate
  Engagement Agreement covering scope, fees, timeline, IP, and
  termination.
- Maintain a register of every NDA signed, with date, contractor,
  scope, and end date. Audit yearly.
- For contractors with cryptocurrency payments, consider adding
  an addendum specifying the wallet address and the chain (BTC,
  USDT TRC-20, USDC ETH).
- For contractors located in jurisdictions with weak IP enforcement
  (e.g. some FATF grey-list jurisdictions), use the engagement
  preferentially via an intermediary with stronger enforcement
  jurisdiction (e.g. LCIA arbitration seat).
